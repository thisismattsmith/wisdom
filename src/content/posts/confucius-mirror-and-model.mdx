<Hero eyebrow="A Verse" title={"The Mirror\nand the Model"}>
  One sentence, two instructions, both pointing inward. Confucius's smallest piece of advice on correction — and the rarest of all responses to other people's faults.
</Hero>

<SourceQuote author="Confucius" work="The Analects, Book 4, Verse 17" year="c. 500 BCE" label="The Verse">
  "When we see men of worth, we should think of equaling them; when we see men of a contrary character, we should turn inwards and examine ourselves."
</SourceQuote>
