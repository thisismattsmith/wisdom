<SourceQuote author="Aristotle" work="Nicomachean Ethics" year="c. 340 BCE">
  "Virtue, then, being of two kinds, intellectual and moral, intellectual virtue in the main owes both its birth and its growth to teaching (for which reason it requires experience and time), while moral virtue comes about as a result of habit… none of the moral virtues arises in us by nature; for nothing that exists by nature can form a habit contrary to its nature."
</SourceQuote>
