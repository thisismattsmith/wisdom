<Hero eyebrow="A Procedure" title="The Rules of Rebuke">
  Maimonides treated correction as a mitzvah — an obligation, not a temptation. But the obligation came with strict procedure: do it privately, do it gently, do it for them, and stop before you wound them.
</Hero>

<SourceQuote author="Maimonides" work="Mishneh Torah, Hilchot De'ot 6:7 (paraphrased)" year="c. 1180">
  Maimonides's core instruction, stated in the opening line of Halacha 6:7, is to rebuke privately, speak patiently and gently, and make clear that you are speaking for the other person's good. The remainder of the chapter elaborates the procedure summarised below; readers are encouraged to consult the linked Sefaria edition for the full Hebrew and English text.
</SourceQuote>
